CNC Pen Plotter

Arduino · GRBL · SolidWorks

I designed and fabricated a fully custom-built 2-axis CNC pen plotter from scratch. I modeled the frame in SolidWorks and 3D printed it in PLA. To drive the motion, I used NEMA 17 stepper motors controlled via an Arduino Nano running GRBL-Servo firmware, utilizing A4988 drivers and a GT2 belt system that I calibrated to 81 steps/mm. For my software workflow, I convert SVG artwork into G-code using svg2gcode, and then stream it to the machine via LaserGRBL. Using this setup, I have successfully reproduced detailed line art, including portrait work and hand-lettered text.

Lithophane Lamp

3D Print · Electronics · FastLED

I built a handmade rechargeable lithophane lamp as a gift. I designed the enclosure in SolidWorks and 3D printed it in PLA. Inside, it houses an 18650 battery that I charge via a TP4056 module and boost to operating voltage using an XL6009E1. For the lighting, I used a WS2812 LED ring driven by an Arduino Nano and the FastLED library, providing 8 selectable color modes. I also added a potentiometer to control the brightness and programmed a long-press random color function. I had to put some significant troubleshooting into resolving LED failures caused by voltage spikes from the boost converter, which I eventually fixed by adding filtering capacitors and fine-tuning the output voltage.

3 Speed Manual Gearbox

3D Print · Gear Ratios · Power Transmission

For my machine design project centered around gearing and power transmission, I naturally decided to build a 3-speed gearbox with a sliding clutch sleeve. My biggest challenge was shaft alignment: I had to design the gear sets so that the center distance was equal across all three ratios, ensuring the three shafts remained parallel regardless of which gear was engaged. Any mismatch would have caused the shafts to skew, introducing power loss and binding. Finally, I designed the housing in SolidWorks and manufactured it using wood and 3D printed PLA components.

Filament Dryer

Thermodynamics · Control Electronics

I built a custom filament drying chamber to improve my 3D print quality. I used a 37L polypropylene container for the enclosure and fitted it with a 120W PTC fan heater as the primary heat source. Inside, I placed three DHT22 sensors at different levels to provide an averaged temperature reading across the full interior volume. To regulate it, I programmed a simple hysteresis control loop running on an Arduino Uno that keeps the internal temperature within ±2°C of the target setpoint. Finally, I power the entire system using a modified 650W desktop PSU.

Bismuth Jewelry

Material Science

For my experimental materials project, I used bismuth that I sourced from a professor. Because bismuth expands as it cools from a melt, it forms distinctive stepped crystals with an iridescent oxide layer. My goal was to shape these crystals into a matching jewelry set—a necklace pendant and earrings. The main challenges I faced were the material's brittleness, controlling the crystal size through my pour timing, and managing thermal shock on the receiving vessel during the melt transfer (I actually had one pot crack on me and had to replace it with a more heat-resistant option). Once I achieved the right crystal sizes, I refined the pieces using sandpaper, working my way from 200 up to 2000 grit. Finally, to preserve the iridescent color—which is a thin oxidation layer vulnerable to further air exposure—I sealed the crystals with UV nail polish. While this slightly dulled some of the color by interfering with the light reflections, my overall result was a wearable, one-of-a-kind set.
